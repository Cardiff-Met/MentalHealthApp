# 1. Introduction

This document presents three user personas and three corresponding usage scenarios for the Personalized Mental Health Support App.

These personas and scenarios help refine product scope, validate the prototype created in Week 2, and prepare for user story development in Weeks 4–5.

# 2. Personas

---

## Persona 1 – Primary User

**Name:** Emily Jones
**Age:** 20
**Background:** Second-year university student studying Psychology in Cardiff. Lives in student accommodation and balances coursework with a part-time job.

### Goals and Motivations
- Manage stress during busy academic periods
- Access mental health resources privately
- Quickly book support sessions when needed

### Frustrations / Pain Points
- Long waiting times for counselling
- Difficulty finding reliable, student-specific support
- Anxiety about privacy when seeking help

### Technical Skill Level
**Medium**
Confident using mobile apps and web platforms but not technical.

### Context of Use
- Uses mobile phone most often
- Logs in during evenings or late at night
- Often under time pressure during assessment periods

### Relationship to the Product
Emily would use the app regularly for mood tracking and resource discovery.
Success looks like:
“I can quickly log how I feel and get helpful suggestions without feeling judged.”

---

## Persona 2 – Secondary User

**Name:** Daniel Evans
**Age:** 24
**Background:** Postgraduate Engineering student. Recently relocated to Cardiff and unfamiliar with local support services.

### Goals and Motivations
- Find local mental health services
- Book appointments efficiently
- Understand what services are available

### Frustrations / Pain Points
- Confusion about where to go for support
- Unclear service eligibility
- Limited time due to research commitments

### Technical Skill Level
**High**
Comfortable navigating digital systems and online booking platforms.

### Context of Use
- Uses laptop during daytime
- Needs structured, reliable information
- Often multitasking between research and coursework

### Relationship to the Product
Daniel would primarily use the booking and service information features.
Success looks like:
“I can identify the right service and book it in minutes.”

---

## Persona 3 – Edge-Case User

**Name:** Aisha Khan
**Age:** 19
**Background:** First-year international student adjusting to university life. English is her second language.

### Goals and Motivations
- Understand how to access help safely
- Feel supported without complex processes
- Use simple, clear interfaces

### Frustrations / Pain Points
- Complex language in service descriptions
- Fear of stigma
- Low confidence in navigating unfamiliar systems

### Technical Skill Level
**Low to Medium**
Uses social media and messaging apps but struggles with complex navigation.

### Context of Use
- Uses smartphone only
- May access the app in shared accommodation
- Concerned about privacy

### Relationship to the Product
Aisha needs reassurance and clarity.
Success looks like:
“I feel supported and can find help without confusion.”

# 3. Usage Scenarios

---

## Scenario 1 – Emily (Primary User)

**Starting Situation:**
Emily has two deadlines next week and feels overwhelmed.

**Trigger:**
She experiences anxiety symptoms late at night.

**Goal:**
Log her mood and receive personalised support suggestions.

**What She Does (High-Level Steps):**
1. Logs into the app
2. Selects “Record Mood”
3. Rates her mood and adds a short description
4. Submits entry
5. Views recommended resources

**Challenge/Constraint:**
She is tired and wants a fast, simple process.

**Outcome (Success):**
She receives tailored coping resources and feels reassured.

---

## Scenario 2 – Daniel (Secondary User)

**Starting Situation:**
Daniel realises he needs professional support after prolonged stress.

**Trigger:**
A friend suggests contacting university counselling.

**Goal:**
Find and book a therapy session.

**What He Does (High-Level Steps):**
1. Logs into the app
2. Navigates to the booking page
3. Reviews available services
4. Selects date and time
5. Confirms booking

**Challenge/Constraint:**
Limited time between academic commitments.

**Outcome (Success):**
Receives booking confirmation and appointment details.

---

## Scenario 3 – Aisha (Edge-Case User)

**Starting Situation:**
Aisha feels isolated during her first semester.

**Trigger:**
She sees a university wellbeing poster recommending the app.

**Goal:**
Explore available support safely and privately.

**What She Does (High-Level Steps):**
1. Registers for an account
2. Logs in
3. Browses the resources page
4. Reads beginner-friendly guidance
5. Saves a helpful resource

**Challenge/Constraint:**
Concerned about privacy and unclear terminology.

**Outcome (Success):**
She understands her options and feels confident seeking help.

# 5. Insights and Backlog Notes

- Clear, simple language is essential for accessibility
- Booking confirmation must be highly visible
- Mood logging should be fast (minimal input friction)
- Privacy reassurance should be emphasised during registration

These insights will inform user story creation in Week 4.
